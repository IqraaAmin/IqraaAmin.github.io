Suddenly shifting from physical classrooms to online learning was not easy. Staying focused at home while keeping up with assignments required a whole new level of discipline. What seemed like a temporary adjustment quickly became one of the most challenging periods of my university journey.

When classes first moved online, I thought the transition would be simple. After all, lectures would still happen and course material would still be available. However, I soon realized that learning from home came with difficulties that I had never experienced before. The biggest challenge was not the coursework itself—it was the environment.

## The Challenges of Learning From Home

Unlike the university campus, home was filled with distractions. It was difficult to create the same learning atmosphere that existed inside a classroom. Staying focused for long online sessions required much more effort than I had expected.

The biggest problem, however, was internet connectivity. There were days when the connection was unstable and lectures would constantly disconnect. Sometimes the audio would break up, making it difficult to understand important explanations. Other times, I would be removed from the session entirely and struggle to reconnect.

This became especially stressful during attendance. I still remember moments when a professor would call my name for attendance, but because of network issues I could not hear properly or respond in time. By the time the connection stabilized, the class had already moved on. It was frustrating because I was present and trying to participate, yet technical problems often made it seem otherwise.

One particular challenge involved sessions conducted by #DrBilal. During some online classes, internet issues would become so severe that I could barely hear parts of the lecture. There were moments when attendance was being taken and the connection was so unstable that I could not clearly understand when my name was called. Experiences like these highlighted how dependent online education was on technology and how difficult learning could become when that technology failed.

## Adapting to a New Reality

As the weeks passed, I realized that complaining about the situation would not change it. Instead, I had to adapt. I started preparing for classes in advance, checking my internet connection whenever possible, downloading materials early, and reviewing lecture notes after each session to make up for anything I might have missed.

This period also taught me the importance of self-discipline. In a physical classroom, the learning environment naturally encourages concentration. Online learning was different. Nobody was physically present to ensure attention or participation. Success depended almost entirely on personal responsibility.

Assignments and deadlines continued regardless of internet problems or technical difficulties. Keeping up with coursework required determination and careful time management. Although the experience was challenging, it helped me develop habits that made me more independent as a student.

## Lessons Beyond the Classroom

Looking back, the online semester was far from ideal. I believe there was a genuine learning loss compared to traditional classroom teaching. Technical issues, limited interaction, and connectivity problems made it harder to fully engage with lectures and discussions.

At the same time, the experience taught me valuable lessons about resilience and adaptability. It showed me that education is not always delivered under perfect conditions and that sometimes students must find ways to continue learning despite obstacles.

While I would always prefer the experience of learning in a physical classroom, surviving the transition to online education gave me greater appreciation for both technology and the opportunities provided by face-to-face learning. It was a difficult period, but it strengthened my ability to adapt to unexpected challenges and continue moving forward despite them.

Today, when I think about that semester, I remember the frustrations, the unstable internet connections, and the missed moments during lectures. But I also remember the persistence it required to keep going. In many ways, those challenges taught me lessons that extended far beyond academics and helped me become a stronger and more determined student.
