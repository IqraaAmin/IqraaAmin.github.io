Object Oriented Programming was one of the most mind-shifting courses of my second semester. Learning about classes, objects, and inheritance completely changed the way I write code. Before studying OOP, programming mostly felt like writing a sequence of instructions to solve a problem. While that worked for smaller programs, I quickly realized that larger projects required a more organized approach.

## A Different Way of Thinking

When OOP was first introduced, concepts like classes, objects, encapsulation, and inheritance seemed confusing. I understood the definitions, but it took some time to understand how they worked together in actual programs.

The turning point came when I started relating these concepts to real-world examples. Thinking of a class as a blueprint and an object as an instance of that blueprint made everything much easier to understand. Instead of focusing only on functions and logic, I began thinking about how data and behavior could be organized into meaningful structures.

This new way of thinking made programming feel much more logical and practical.

## Learning Through Practice

Like most programming concepts, OOP only started making sense when I used it in lab assignments and projects. Creating my first classes, defining attributes, and writing methods helped me understand the purpose behind the theory.

Inheritance was one of the most interesting concepts for me. The ability to reuse and extend existing code showed me how developers manage large software projects efficiently. It reduced repetition and made programs cleaner and easier to maintain.

Of course, there were plenty of mistakes along the way. Some programs refused to work, and debugging became a regular part of the learning process. However, every challenge helped strengthen my understanding of OOP principles.

## Why It Matters

By the end of the semester, I realized that Object Oriented Programming was much more than just another course topic. It changed how I approach software development and taught me the importance of writing structured, reusable, and maintainable code.

Many modern programming languages and frameworks rely heavily on OOP concepts, so learning them felt like an important step forward in my journey as a Computer Engineering student.

Looking back, OOP was one of the subjects that had the greatest impact on my programming mindset. It taught me that good code is not just about making a program work—it's about designing solutions that remain organized, scalable, and easy to understand long after they are written.
